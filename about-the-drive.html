About the Drive

How was it started?

I have been tutoring at Project Read for 5 years. Over the course of helping children with math, reading, science, and all other subjects, I noticed that the children learned better through different learning styles. Because Project Read is a literacy agency, they help children who are reading below their grade level. Every day, after a child is done with their homework, they read a couple of books at their level to further their learning. I have always been an avid reader and a bookworm since I was a child, and I wanted to foster the children's love of reading by adding to their collection. So, I combined what I had noticed with passion for helping these children love reading and aimed to start a book drive to specifically collect different kinds of books for different types of learners.

What are the different types of learning? What types of books were collected?

According to some research and what I noticed from tutoring, there are children who learn visually, audially, kinesthetically, and those who learn by writing.

Visual Learners: I collected books with images inside and large print.


Audial learners: I collected books with audio tapes and CDs.


Kinesthetic learners: I collected books with textured surfaces or pages.


Learners who prefer writing: I collected books with spaces inside for writing phrases or letters.

Who does it benefit?

All the books were donated to Project Read, a tutoring agency.

How many books were collected?

Overall, I'm so happy that I collected 1,024 books! Check out the "Story of My Drive" page for details.
